Top Leaders of Eldoret

Key political figures shaping the future of our city

Governor Jonathan Bii

Governor of Uasin Gishu County

Serving as the second Governor of Uasin Gishu County, Jonathan Bii focuses on agricultural development, youth empowerment, and infrastructure improvement. His administration has prioritized water accessibility and healthcare services across the county. With a background in business management, he brings private sector efficiency to public service. His leadership continues Eldoret's legacy as the economic hub of the North Rift region.

Senator Jackson Mandago

Senator of Uasin Gishu County

As the former Governor and current Senator, Jackson Mandago has been instrumental in shaping Eldoret's development agenda. He championed the establishment of the Eldoret International Airport and various educational initiatives. His legislative focus includes county revenue allocation and devolution implementation. With extensive experience in county governance, he provides crucial oversight and represents county interests at the national level.

Deputy Governor Evans Kapkea

Deputy Governor of Uasin Gishu County

Deputy Governor Evans Kapkea serves as the second-in-command of Uasin Gishu County, working closely with Governor Jonathan Bii to implement the county's development agenda. He focuses on agricultural innovation, youth empowerment programs, and public service delivery improvement. With extensive experience in public administration, Kapkea plays a crucial role in overseeing daily county operations and coordinating inter-departmental functions. His dedication to grassroots development ensures that government services reach all residents of Eldoret and surrounding areas effectively.

Woman Rep Gladys Boss Shollei

Uasin Gishu County Women Representative

Gladys Boss Shollei serves as the Women Representative for Uasin Gishu County, focusing on gender equality, women empowerment, and child welfare programs. She has initiated several projects supporting women entrepreneurs and providing educational scholarships for girls. Her advocacy extends to improved maternal healthcare services and protection of women's rights. Through her leadership, she continues to champion the role of women in Eldoret's development.
