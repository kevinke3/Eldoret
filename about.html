About Eldoret

Discover the heartbeat of Kenya's North Rift region

Introduction

Eldoret is the fifth-largest city in Kenya and the capital of Uasin Gishu County. Situated at an elevation of 2,100 meters above sea level, it serves as the economic hub of the North Rift region.

Known as the "City of Champions," Eldoret has produced some of the world's greatest long-distance runners and is a major agricultural center specializing in wheat, maize, and dairy farming.

History & Growth

Founded in 1910 by South African settlers, Eldoret began as a small farming community. The name originates from the Maasai word "eldore" meaning "stony river," referring to the Sosiani River that flows through the city.

The town grew rapidly with the extension of the Uganda Railway and was officially gazetted as a municipality in 1961. Today, it's home to over 400,000 residents and continues to expand as an educational and commercial center.

Economy & Agriculture

Eldoret is the breadbasket of Kenya, with vast wheat and maize farms surrounding the city. The Moi University and Eldoret International Airport contribute significantly to the local economy.

Key industries include agro-processing, textiles, and manufacturing. The city hosts several major banks, insurance companies, and the largest milk processing plant in East Africa.

Sports Culture

Eldoret is synonymous with world-class athletics. The high-altitude training grounds have produced Olympic champions like Eliud Kipchoge, David Rudisha, and Tegla Loroupe.

The city hosts numerous athletic events and training camps throughout the year. It's home to the Kipchoge Keino Stadium and numerous world-class training facilities that attract athletes from around the globe.
